The Delhi Sultanate

Ilbari/Slave Dynasty (1206-90)

Qutubuddin Aibak (1206-10)

  • Originally a slave Did not belong to the Ilbari tribe of Turks
  • Was Viceroy of Muhammad Ghori
  • He was given the title of Lakh-Baksh for his generosity
  • He constructed Qwwuat-ul-Islam in Delhi and Adhai din ka Jhopra in Ajmer
  • Started construction of Qutub Minar in honor of great sufi saint Khawaja Qutub-ud-din Bakhtiyar Kaki
  • Died while playing polo (chaugan)

Aram Shah

  • Aibak's son was incompetent as a ruler
  • Nobles invited Aibak's son-in-law, the governer of Badaun Iltutmish to take up the throne

    • Iltutmish (1210-36)

    • He is considered to be the real consolidator of Turkish conquest in India
    • In the early years of his reign, he supressed the revolts of ambitious nobles
    • In 1215 he defeated Yaldoz who established himself as an independent ruler of Ghazni
    • He sent expeditions against the Rajputs in Ranathambore, Gwalior,Ajmer and Malwa
    • During his reign Chenghiz Khan made his first appearance in India. Iltutmish saved the sultanate from his wrath by not shielding Khwarizm Shah and Jalaluddin Mangobani who he was chasing
    • He got his authority recognised from the Caliph of Baghdad
    • Completed construction of the Qutub Minar
    • Started the Iqta system, in which land was distributed to officers in lieu of salary
    • Contructed a corp of 40 loyal slave Amins, known as Turkan-i-chahalgani or Chalisa
    • Introduced Silver Tankhas and copper Jital
    • Known as the Father of tomb building in India

    Rukunuddin Firoz (1236)

    • Iltutmish appointed Raziya his daughter as his successor
    • Nobles could not reconcile with the idea of a woman ruling over them
    • They put Iltutmish's son Rukunuddin Firoz on the throne
    • He turned out to be worthless, leaving government work in the hands of his mother Shah Turkan
    • Finally throne was given to Raziya when he left to Awadh to quell a rebellion against him

    Raziya Sultan (1236-40)

    • First and last female Musim ruler in medieval India
    • Quelled the rebellion in Multan, Lahore and Jhansi
    • The wazir Nizam-ul-mul Junaidi who opposed Raziya, was defeated by her
    • Sent expeditions to ranathambore to control the rajputs
    • Her appointing a non turk named Yakut to the high office led to opposition
    • Altunia the governor of Bhatinda refused to accept rule of Raziya. Raziya accompanied by Yakut marched against Altunia
    • Altunia killed Yakut and Imprisoned Raziya, subsequently Raziya married Altunia
    • Raziya was the victim of a conspiracy and was assassinated by the Jats near Kaithal (Haryana) in 1230
    • Battle of succession continued in which the following rulers ruled insignificantly

    Giyasuddin Balban (1265-1286)

    • Ascended the throne after killing all member of Iltutmish's family
    • He was a Member of the Chalisa or Chehalgani, but he broke the power of the chehalgani and restored prestige to the crown
    • Created a strong and centralized military department Diwan-i-arz
    • Ordered seperation of millitary from finance
    • Declared sultan as the reprasentative of God on Earth
    • Took the title of Zil-i-Ilahi, Introduced Presian etiquette of Sijda and Paibos
    • Gave up drinking, refused to laugh and joke in the court
    • Adopted the policy of Blood and Iron
    • Started the festival of Nauroz

    Kaiqubad (1287-90)

    • Grandson of Balban, he was put on the throne by Fakruddin, the kotwal of Delhi
    • He was killed by the Khaljis
    • This marked the end of the Ilbari dynasty and start of Khalji dynasty

    The Khalji Dynasty (1290-1320)

    • Overthrew the incompetent successors of Balban
    • End of Turkish monopoly

    Jalaluddin Khilji (1290-96)

    • He adopted the Policy of tolerance and avoiding harsh punishments. Rejected the idea of India becoming a Islamic state
    • Invasion of Devagiri (1294) by his son-in-law and cousin Alauddin Khilji
    • Married his daughter with Ulugh Khan, descendant of Chengiz Khan to earn his goodwill

    Alauddin Khilji (1296-1316)

    • Conquest
    • Admistrative Reforms
    • Revenue Reforms
    • Market/Economic Reforms

    Successors Of Alauddin Khilji

    • Malik Kafur seized the throne but could not rule for long and nominated Alauddin's 16th son Shihabuddin as the king
    • Shihabuddin deposed of by Qutubuddin Mubarak Shah
    • Nasiruddin Shah killed Qutubuddin Mubarak Shah but was himself killed byGhazi Malik
    • Thus Khilji dyansty ended and Tughlaq dynasty started

    The Tughlaq Dynasty (1320-1414)

    Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq (1320-1325)

    • Ghazi Malik or Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq founded the Tughlaq dynasty or Quarana Turks dynasty
    • First Delhi Sultan to take up the title of Ghazi
    • liberalized Allauddin's administrative policies and took keen interest in construction of Canals
    • Formulated the Famine policy, efficient Postal service was restored
    • Sent his son Jauna Khan to re-establish authority in Warangal (Kakatiya) and Madurai (Pandyas)
    • Built the capital of Tughlaqabad
    • Died by Falling from high pavilion
    • Moroccan traveller Ibn-Battuta was in Delhi at the time He recorded the contemporary Indian scence in his Safarnamah

    Muhammed Bin Tughlaq (1325-1351)

    • Jauna Khan ascended the thone under the name of Muhammad Bin Tughlaq
    • Most Learned and extraorninary sultan ever, but his policies never got success because of his hastyness
    • Diwan-i-Kohi the department of agriculture was set up
    • Built the city of Jahapanah
    • Five Ambitious projects
      • Taxation in the doab (1326)
      • Transfer of capital (1327) from Delhi to Deogiri(Daulatabad)
      • Introduction of token(bronze) currency (1329)
      • The Khurasan(Iraq) expedition (1329)
      • Quarachil expedition (1330) :- Died in Thatta

    Firoz Shah Tughlaq (1351-1388)

    • Appeased nobility due to imminent danger of breaking up of state
    • Made the Iqta system hereditary
    • Soldiers were not paid by cash, but by assignments on land revenue Vajeha
    • A lot of attention to irrigation, He imposed Water tax Haq-i-Sharb and Hasil-i-Sharb
    • Built Two Canals
      • Sutlej to Ghaggar
      • Yamuna to Hissar
    • Encouraged the practice of slavery, selected young boys from conquered territory for this purpose. Diwan-i-Bandagon was the department for slavery
    • Built new cities of Jaunpur Hissar Firozabad (Renamed Pandua) Fatehabad
    • Diwan-i-Khairat for marriage of poor girls, also set up a hospital Darul-Shifa
    • Brought two Ashokan pillars to Delhi, Repaired Qutub Minar
    • Taxation
      • Kharaj : 1/10 th of land produce
      • Zakat : 2.5% Property tax by muslims
      • Jaziya : Tax on Hindus only also Brahmins
      • Khams : 1/6 th of te captured booty in war
    • Mathura was destroyed
