The Provintial Kingdoms

Gujarat

  • Disintegrated from Delhi in 1397 under Zaffar Khan who took the title of Sultan Muzaffar Shah
  • Ahmed Shah I built a new city Ahmedabad and also built Jama Masjid and Tin Darwaza at Ahmedabad
  • Mahmud Beghra was another prominent ruler, under whose rule the Portuguese set up a factory
  • Sanskrit scholar Udayraja was his court poet

Malwa

  • Annexed by Alauddin Khilji in AD 1305
  • Dilawar Khan Ghuri declared independence in 1435
  • Hasan Shah built the Jama Masjid, Hindol Mahal and Jahaz Mahal at Mandu
  • Annexed to the Mughal state in 1562

Kashmir

  • Zainalubedin was the greatest ruler of Kashmir, known as Badshah and the Akbar Of Kashmir
  • Introduced art of shawl making, constructed Zaina lake and artificial island in the wular lake
  • Later ruled by Chak dynasty who submitted to Akbar in 1562

Mewar

  • Capital city of Chittor was captured by Alauddin Khilji in 1303 but Rajput rule was restored by Rana Hamir in 1326
  • Rana Kumbh Karn was the greatest ruler of Mewar
  • He built the famous Vijay Stambh in Chittor to commemorate his vistory over Mahmud Khilji of Malwa
  • Mandan was his court poet
  • Rana Sangram Singh defeated Mahmun II of Malwa and Ibrahim Lodi but he was defeated by Babur in the battle of Khanwa (1527)

Bengal

  • Dissintigrated out of the sultanate from the time of the Mohammed Bin Tughlaq
  • In 1432 Ilias Khan founded the Ilias Sahi Dynasty
  • Poet Maladhar Basu compiler of Sri-Krishna Bijay was patronised by the sultans and given the title of Gunaraja Khan

Vijaynagar Kingdom (1336-1580)

  • Founded by Harihara I and Bukka
  • Originally Feudatories of the Kakatiyas
  • Brought to the centre and converted to Islam by Mohammed Bin Tughlaq
  • Sent to south to control the rebellion
  • Reconverted to Hindu under influence of Bhakti saint Vidyaranya

Harihara I

  • Conflict with Bahmani kingdom for supremacy over the Raichur Doab

Bukka I

  • Renamed city of Vidyanagar to Vijaynagar
  • Restored peace between warring Vaishnavs and Jains

Devaraya I

  • Italian traveller Nicolo Conti visited kingdom during his reign
  • Constructed a dam across Tungabhadra
  • Srinath was his court poet who wrote Haravilasam
  • There was a Pearl Hall to honour men of emminence

Devaraya II

  • Considered an incarnation of Indra
  • Wrote Mahanayaka Suddhanidhi
  • Took the title of Ganga Betekara (Elephant Hunter)
  • Persina ambassador Abdur Razzaq and envoy of Shah Rukh visited his court

The Suluvas

  • Suluva Narasimgha was the founder
  • Vasco Da Gama came to India (calicut) in 1498 during the reogn of Immadi

The Tuluvas

  • Vira Narasimha killed Immadi and established the Tuluva dynasty
  • Krishnadevaraya (1509-29)
    • Greatest ruler of this dynasty
    • Domingo Peas, Barbossa Albukirk, Frair Louis all came during his time
    • Golden age of Telugu literature
    • His political ideas are written in Amuktamalyada he also wrote Sanskrit drama Jambavati Kalyanam
    • Known as Andhra Pitamaha, Andhra Bhoja and Abhinava Bhoja
    • Eight great poets of telugu the Ashtadigwaja adorned his court
  • Achutya Raja was his successor. Portuguese traveller Fernao Nuniz came during his reign
  • Sadasiv was the last ruler of this dynasty. Ceaser Frederick the Portuguese traveller visited his court
  • He was defeated in the Battle of Talikota (1565) by an alliance of Bujapur ,Golkonda and Bidar

Bahamani Kingdom (1347-1527)

  • Founded by Hasan Gangu (Ismail Mukh) under the title of Alauddin Hasan Bahaman Shah
  • Mahmud Shah I
  • Firoz Shah
    • Most remarkable ruler
    • Defeated Devaraya I
    • Built Observatory at Daulatabad
    • Founded the city of Firozabad
  • Ahmed Shah I known as Wali or saint, shifted capital to Bidar
  • Alauddin II and Humayun (Zalim)
  • Last ruler was Kalim Ullah Shah
  • After breakup of Bahmani kingdom, five seperate Muslim states were formed
