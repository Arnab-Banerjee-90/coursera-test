Aryan and Vedic age (1500-600 B.C)

Origins

  • Popular belief is that Aryans migrated from central Asia to Indian subcontinent in several waves from 2000-1500 B.C
  • Boghazkhai inscription (Asia minor,Turkey) mentions the four vedic Gods (Indra,Varuna,Mitra,Nasatyas),proving the central migration theory

Rigvedic or early Vedic period (1500-1000 B.C)

  • Rivers Known :- Ganga,Yamuna,Saraswati(Nanditara)
  • Warfare :-
    • Dasyuhatya:- Slaughter of Dasyus (Original inhabitants) by the Aryans (Mentioned in Rigveda)
    • Dasarajna:-
      • Fought on the banks of Parushni (Ravi) river.
      • Fought Between Alliance of Sudas (son of Divodas) and Bharata king of Tristu family guided by Vasistha and an alliance of ten tribes guided by Viswamitra.
      • Alliance of Sudas (son of Divodas) and Bharata king of tristu family guided by Vasistha were victorious
      • Leader of the ten tribe alliance Purusukta was killed
  • Economy :-
    • Mixed Economy
    • Better knowledge in agriculture,predominantly pastoral
    • Cow was the standard unit of exchange
    • Gold coins like Niksha,Krishna,Satmana were also used
  • Polity :-
    • King elected by tribal assembly :- Samiti
    • Council of elders :- Sabha
    • Religious advisor to the King :- Purohita
  • Society :-
    • Primary loyalty was to the tribe :- Jana or Vis
    • Society divided into four varnas Brahmins,Khatriyas,Vaishyas,Sudras
    • Women were highly placed in society
  • Gods :-
    • Believed in nature worship, did not erect temple and worship idols
    • Soma:- God of plants Ninth mandala of Rigveda attributed to Soma
    • Usha and Aditi:- Represents appearance of dawn
  • Rituals :-
    • Rajasuya: To strenghten king's influence, it was supposed to confer supreme power to the king
    • Aswamedha: To have unquestioned control over the area
    • Vajpeya: Chariot race in which the royal chariot was made to win a race against his kinsmen

Later Vedic Age (Painted Grayware Phase) (1000-600 B.C)

  • Rivers Known :- Narmada, Sadanira
  • Territorial Division Of India :- Aryavarta,Madhyadesa and Dakshinpatha
  • Economy :-
    • Agriculture was the chief economic activity
    • New occupations emerged e.g fisherman,washerman,dyers,door-keepers and footmen
    • Tin silver and Iron was now known to people
    • Merchants were organised into guilds
  • Polity :-
    • Formation of large kingdoms, The trem Rashtra emerged, kingship became hereditary
    • Assembly lost importance, royal power inceresed
    • Women no longer permitted to join assemblies
    • Formation of judiciary,kings administered the criminal court
  • Society :-
    • Fourfold division became hereditary intead of occupational
    • Ashram system was formed
    • Position of women decilned, polygamy became frequent
  • Religion :-
    • Rituals became more importnat in the cult of sacrifice
    • Prajapati became the Supreme God
    • Pushan became the god of the Shudras
    • Towards the end of the Vedic age a section of the society resented the priestly domination

Vedic Literature

  • Rig veda :-
    • Oldest religious text
    • Collections of hymns, around 1700 B.C,contains 1028 Hymns divided into 10 mandalas
    • II to VII are the earliest mandalas
    • VIII mandala is ascribed to Kanvas and Angiras
    • IX mandala is compilation of Soma hymns
    • X th mandals contains the famous purushashukta hymn which explains that the four varnas were born from the mouth arms thighs and feet of the creator Bramha
    • III mandala contains the Gayatri Mantra, compiled in the praise of Savitri
  • Sama veda :-
    • Collection of melodies, 'saman' means melody
    • Contains the Dhrupad Ragas
    • Recited by Udgatri at soma Sacrifice
  • Yajur veda :-
    • Deals with procedures of performance of sacrifices
    • Two texts of Yajurveda, Shukla and Krishna
  • Atharva veda :-
    • Contains charms and spells to ward off evil spirits and disease
  • Upvedas :-
    • Dhanurveda-upveda of Yajurveda deals with the art of warfare
    • Gandharvaveda-upveda of samveda deals with art and music
    • Shastrashastra-associated with Atharvaveda deals with military technology
    • Arurveda-upveda of Rigveda deals with medicine
  • Vedangas (Limbs of the vedas) :-
    • Shiksha
    • Kalpa-Rituals
    • Vyakarana (Ashtyadhayi written by Panini on Vyakarana on 4th century BC)
    • Nirukta-Etymology
    • Chhanda-Metrics
    • Jyotisha
  • Upanishads (Vedarita) :-
    • Knowledge acquired by sitting close to the teacher
    • spiritual and philosophical in nature explains the aim of the vedas
    • 108 Upanishads period of 800-500 B.C. 11 are predominant and are called Moksha Upanishads
  • Aryanyaks :-
    • Sages dwelling in the forests explained the vedic scriptures to their pupils
    • Forms the concluding part of the Brahmanas, provides the details of rishis living in the jungles
  • Brahmanas :-
    • Prose commentaries on various vedic hymns
    • Explains the vedas in an orthodox way
    • Most important is the satapatha brahmana attached to Yajurveda
  • Smritis :-
    • Law books written in sloka form also called Dharma Shastra
    • Manav Dharma Shastra (Manu Smriti) is the oldest and most famous
