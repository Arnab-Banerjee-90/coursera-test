Pre Mauryan Age (600-321 B.C)

Origins

  • Material advantages of using iron
  • Agricultural surplus taxed to finance military and admistrative needs
  • Many janapads sprung up in the 6th century B.C, larger of which were called the Mahajanapads

The Magadha Empire

Origins

  • 6th to 4th century B.C saw the struggle for supermacy among the mahajanpads
  • Magadha,Kosala,Vatsa and Avanti were the most powerful
  • Magadha emerged as the most powerful kindom
  • Jarasandha and Brihadratha were the founders of Magadha
    • Growth started under the Haryankas
    • Expanded under the Shishunagas and Nandas
    • Peaked under the Mauryas

Haryanka Dynasty

Bimbisara (544-492 B.C)

  • Built the capital city of Rajgir (Girivraja)
  • Contemporary of Gautam Buddha and Mahavira
  • First king to have a standing army
  • Defeated Anga king Brahmadutta, strenghened his postion via matrimonial alliances with Lichhavi and Koshal
  • Sent Jivak (personal physician) to rival Avanti king Chadrapradyot Mahasena of Ujjain to cure jaundice
  • Gandhar ruler of taxila sent an embassy to Bimbisara

Ajatasatru (492-460 B.C)

  • Gained complete control of Kasi(Girivraja)
  • Defeated his maternal uncle king of Kosala
  • Destroyed Vaishali
  • Sunidha and Vatsakar diplomatic advisors
  • Fortified Rajgir from Avanti threat
  • Patronised the first Buddhist council,Buddha died during his reign

Udayin (460-444 B.C)

  • Moved capital to pataliputra
  • Succedded by wesk rulers Aniruddha,Munda and Naga Dasaka

Shishunaga Dynasty (412-344 B.C)

Shishunaga

  • Ministed of Naga Dasaka, elected by the people
  • Conquered the Pradyota dynasty of Avanti
  • Temporarily shifted capital to Vaishali

Kalashoka

  • Shifted capital back to pataliputra
  • Convened the second Buddhist Council in Vaishali (383 B.C

Nanda Dynasty (First non Khatriya dynasty) (344-323 B.C)

Mahapadmananda

  • Conquered Kosala and Kalinga
  • His titles were Ekarata,Eka-Chattra,Sarvakshatrakantaka (uprooter of Khatriyas),Ugrasena
  • Succeeded by eight sons, last of them were Dhanananda

Dhanananda

  • Alexander invaded north-western India during his reign (326 B.C)
  • Referred to as Agrammes or Xan-drames in Greek texts
  • Chandragupta Maurya overthrew Dhanananda and Maurya Empire established in 321 B.C
