Post Mauryan Age (185 B.C - 319 A.D)

Maurya Successors

  • Native States
  • Foriegn States

Native States

Shunga Dynasty (185 - 73 B.C)

  • Pushyamitra Shunga ruled from Vidisha,defeated Bactrian king Dematrius
  • Known as prosecutor of Buddhism
  • Greek ambassador Heliodorus visited court of fifth Shunga king Bhagabhadra
  • Shunga age marked the revival of the Hindu culture and Sanskrit language,Sanskrit grammarian patanjali was patronised
  • Manusmriti compiled diring this period

Kanva Dynasty (73 - 28 B.C)

  • Minor dynasty founded by Vasudeva, killed last Shunga king Debavuti
  • All rulers were Brahmin
  • Last ruler Susarman, killed by Andhra(satvahana) king Simuka

Satvahana Dynasty (28 B.C - 198 A.D)

  • Simuka was the founder of this empire
  • Gautami putra Satkarni (106-130 A.D) was the greatest ruler of this dynasty. He assumed the title of raja-raja and maha-raja
  • His capital was at Paithan or Pratisthan on the banks of Godavari river.He boasted that he was a true brahmin.All Satvahana rulers claimed to be brahmins
  • Vasistha putra Satkarni married the daughter of Saka ruler Rudradaman but was twice defeated by him
  • Yajna Sri Satkarni recovered konkan and Malwa from Saka rulers

Foreign States

The Indo-Greeks (200 - 145 B.C)

  • They were the first to invade, the weak mauryans were too weak to resist
  • Menander (165-145 B.C) was the most famous Indo-Greek ruler
    • His capital was in Sakala (present Sialkot)
    • Converted to Buddhism by Nagasena, their conversations recorded in the book Milindapanho
  • They were the first rulers in India to issue coins

The Sakas (1st - 4th Century A.D)

  • Most famous Saka ruler was Rudradaman
    • Repaired Sudarshana lake (Constructed by Pushyagupta under Chandragupta Maurya)
    • Issued firsted ever longest inscription in Chaste Sanskrit at Junagarh

The Parthians (1st - 3rd Century A.D)

  • Originally from Iran,they moved to India and replaced the Sakas
  • Occupied only a small portion of north-western India
  • Most famous king was Gondophernes in whose reign St Thomas came to India to spread Christanity

    Kanishka (78 - 101 A.D)

    • Known as Second Ashoka
    • Started an era from 78 A.D known as Saka era or Sakabda
    • Two capitals Purushpur and Mathura
    • Patronised the fourth Buddhist Council in Kashmir
    • Patronised the following persons
      • Nagarjuna - Madhyamik
      • Ashwaghosha - Buddhacharit
      • Vasumitra - Chairman of the fourth Buddhist council
      • Charaka - Charakasamhita
